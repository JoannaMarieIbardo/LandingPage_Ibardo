Teenage Years

When I was in my teenage years, around 7th to 10th grade, my classmates and I would hang out together after school.1 We would go to KTVs or the mall, just to play in the arcades, because we had free time to do whatever we wanted.2 After playing, we would go out to eat and enjoy each other’s company. Those were some of the best moments of my teenage years.3 We had so much fun, My teenage years were also filled with school competitions, such as dancing and role-playing.4 I was very outgoing back then, never shy to join any activity. I loved being involved in everything, whether it was dancing, acting, or just having fun with friends.5 Now, I can’t imagine doing those things anymore. My confidence during my teenage years was something I took for granted, but now I feel different.6 I also joined the varsity badminton team in high school. We competed in intramurals, playing against different schools. I participated in both singles and doubles events.7 Sometimes we won gold, sometimes silver, and those wins made me proud. I worked hard with my teammates, and the experiences taught me a lot about teamwork.8 Looking back, I miss the energy and excitement of those days. I miss the competitions, the fun with friends, and the freedom to do whatever I wanted.9 Now, I feel like I can’t do the same things I used to do. My teenage years were full of unforgettable moments that I will always remember.10 I learned a lot during those years, and they shaped the person I am today. Though I can’t go back to those times, I cherish those memories and the lessons I learned.11
