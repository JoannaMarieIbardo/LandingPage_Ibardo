Childhood Years

Here’s my childhood story. I loved playing outside with my childhood friends.1 We played Chinese garter, piko, tagu-taguan, and many other Filipino games.2 It was always fun, and we never got tired of running around. We laughed, shouted, and competed with each other every day.3 No matter how hot or rainy it was, we always found a way to play.4 I still remember when it was getting darker outside, it would be time to go home.5 But I never wanted to stop playing. Every evening, my lolo would come looking for me.6 He would call me angrily, “Uwi na, Kababae mong tao, nasa labas ka pa!”7 The moment I saw him, I would quickly run back home.8 At that time, I felt mad whenever I was forced to stop playing.9 I thought it was unfair that I had to go home while my friends could still play.10 I would stomp my feet and pout, wishing I could stay outside longer.11 But now that I am grown up, I understand why my lolo was so strict.12 He was only protecting me and making sure I was safe.13 Looking back, those childhood moments make me smile.14 I miss the days when life was simple, and all I cared about was playing.15 The friendships I made during those years were priceless.16 Those memories will always stay with me. Even though I didn’t realize it back then, my childhood was truly a happy one.17
